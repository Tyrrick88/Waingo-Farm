Our Story

A farm built like an operation, run like a family

Waingo Farm started in 2009 as a single onion plot on the slopes outside Nyeri. Today it spans 240 hectares of crops and pasture — but the instinct that built it hasn't changed: know your soil, know your animals, and never stop measuring.

We farm at the intersection of tradition and precision. Drip lines run beside hand-checked furrows. Every dairy cow has a health record. Every plot has a planting log. It's not farming for the sake of technology — it's technology in service of better food, grown honestly.

Sustainable by design

Crop rotation, composting, and water-efficient irrigation across every plot.

Measured, not guessed

Soil, yield, and herd data tracked daily across the whole operation.

People-first

68 full-time workers, fair wages, and on-site training programs.

Milestones

How We Farm

Modern methods, applied deliberately

Five systems we've invested in because they measurably improve yield, welfare, or sustainability — not because they're trendy.

01

Drip Irrigation

Precision water delivery across all vegetable and onion plots, cutting water use by 40% versus flood irrigation while improving yield consistency.

02

Climate-Controlled Greenhouses

Four greenhouse units allow year-round vegetable production, independent of rainy-season volatility.

03

Organic Input Programs

98% of crop plots are managed with composted manure and biological pest control instead of synthetic inputs.

04

Precision Soil Monitoring

Soil moisture and pH sensors across key plots feed directly into our planting and irrigation schedules.

05

Rotational Grazing

Pasture is divided into paddocks and rotated weekly, keeping grass healthy and reducing parasite load in livestock.
